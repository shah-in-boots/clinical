Admitted for perforated appendicitis
Developed paroxysmal CHB
Underwent MICRA implant with perforation

Course

  • 84 year old female with no prior medical history except mild memory loss
  • Presents for perforated appendicitis, conservatively managed
  • Intermittent episodes of CHB
  • Grade 1 diastology and normal LV function on surface echocardiogram
  • After stabilization of appendicitis, underwent MICRA implant

Telemetry findings show complete heart block intermittently

MICRA implantation

  • Right common femoral vein access, US guided
  • 8F sheath with pre-close x 2
  • Standard MICRA implant technique

MICRA when taken out showed exposed tines. Lock was still in place. No notable force issues on implant. However did not pull suture back and re-lock after flushing with contrast.

Overnight

  • Monitored overnight in ICU
  • Started on colchicine empirically
  • Received several point-of-care echocardiograms
  • Revealed stable to slight increase in size between lab and arrival in ICU
  • Resolution and coagulation by following morning

Considerations

  • MICRA perforation risk is primarily in elderly, low body mass, women, and kidney disease
  • Additional risks include tine exposure with flushing, may help to re-lock tines after flushing
  • Etiology of effusion from tine puncture or disk/cup perforation of RV?
