Tachycardia #1

Pacing during sinus rhythm at approximately the same cycle length as tachycardia. The AH interval is approximately 80 ms. We can then compare AH interval during tachycardia.

The tachcyardia, of a similar cycle length, has an AH interval that is approximately the same as that during sinus pacing. About 71 ms.

The surface leads show change in morphology, suggesting left bundle aberrancy. The AH interval is the same during this however, approximately 90 miliseconds.

Delta AH Maneuver

Indication

Long RP tachycardia can be challenging, particularly with posteroseptal accessory pathways and right atrial tachycardias.

Useful for differentiating between atypical AVNRT from ORT/AT

  1. Atypical AVNRT
  2. Atrial tachycardia
  3. ORT with slowly conducting accessory pathway

Utilization

  • Long RP tachycardia, RP \(\ge\) PR, e.g. VA \(\gt\) 70 ms
  • Narrow complex, regular tachycardia, with 1:1 conduction
  • 1:1 AV conduction when pacing during sinus at TCL
  • Visible His bundle recording

Limitations

  • AV block during pacing at TCL
  • Does not exclude dual AV nodal physiology during ORT or AT (leading to longer \(\Delta AH\)

Technique

  1. HRA pacing during sinus rhythm at \(PCL = TCL\), with measurement of AH interval
  2. Measurement of AH interval during tachycardia

Interpretation:

\(\Delta AH = | AH_{S1} - AH_{tach}|\) \(\Delta AH > 40 ms \Rightarrow atypical\ AVNRT\) \(\Delta AH < 20 ms \Rightarrow ORT \lor AT\)

Schematic showing difference for the AH interval for atypical AVNRT and atrial pacing by Man et al. 1995
